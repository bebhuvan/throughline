# Illustration Specs — Strait of Hormuz Explainer

These are detailed briefs for bespoke editorial illustrations. Style: refined editorial silhouettes. Warm paper palette. Clean linework. No clip art. No 3D renders. Think New York Times graphics desk or Guardian visual team.

All illustrations should use the project's color palette: ink, ink-secondary, accent, viz-blue, viz-amber, viz-green, paper-warm.

---

## 1. VLCC Supertanker — Scale & Anatomy

**Placement:** "A corridor narrower than a city boulevard" section, after the paragraph about VLCC dimensions.

**What to show:**
- Side profile silhouette of a Very Large Crude Carrier (VLCC)
- Length: 330 metres. Width: 60 metres. Draft: 22 metres.
- Cargo: 2 million barrels of crude oil
- Scale comparisons alongside it:
  - Eiffel Tower (330m) — same height as the tanker is long
  - Football pitch (105m) — three football pitches end to end
  - Boeing 747 (70m) — just over a quarter of the tanker length
- Cutaway showing crude oil cargo tanks (typically 15-17 tanks)
- Waterline clearly marked showing the 22m draft

**Style:** Clean side elevation. Warm grey hull. Accent color for waterline and cargo indication. Scale figures in ink-secondary. Sans-serif labels.

**Size:** Full breakout width (960px). Aspect ratio ~3:1 landscape.

## 2. The Strait from Above — Tactical Geography

**Placement:** After the HormuzMap component, as an inset detail view.

**What to show:**
- Stylized bird's-eye view of the narrowest point of the strait
- Iranian coastline (top/north) with:
  - Missile battery positions (small chevron icons)
  - IRGC fast-boat berths (small dock icons)
  - Qeshm Island, Hormuz Island, Larak Island labeled
- Omani coastline (bottom/south) with Musandam Peninsula tip
- Two shipping lanes clearly marked:
  - Inbound lane (3.2 km wide) along Omani coast — marked with subtle ship silhouettes heading into the Gulf
  - Buffer zone (3.2 km) in the middle — hatched/striped
  - Outbound lane (3.2 km) along Iranian coast — ships heading out
- Total width annotation: 10 km navigable corridor
- Coast-to-coast annotation: 39 km
- Depth markers: <25m near Iranian coast, 60-100m in channels
- A VLCC to scale in one of the lanes (showing how small it looks in the lane, but how the lane only fits a few ships abreast)

**Style:** Muted topographic feel. Iranian coast in warm sand tones. Water in paper-alt blue. Shipping lanes in subtle viz-blue. Threat positions in viz-red (small, understated). Labels in Inter.

**Size:** Full breakout width (960px). Aspect ratio ~4:3.

## 3. The Cascade Chain — From Wellhead to Kitchen

**Placement:** At the start of "From crude oil to your kitchen" section, before the cascade stat boxes.

**What to show:**
A horizontal illustrated chain showing the physical journey from extraction to consumption, with each step connected by flowing lines:

1. **Oil wellhead** (offshore platform in the Gulf) →
2. **Tanker** (VLCC silhouette in the strait) →
3. **Refinery** (distillation towers) →
4. **Gas pipeline / LNG tanker** →
5. **Fertilizer plant** (industrial building) →
6. **Farm field** (wheat/rice) →
7. **Truck** (delivery) →
8. **Kitchen stove / gas cylinder** (LPG)

A second branch splits from the refinery:
- Refinery → **Petrochemical plant** → **Plastic products** (bottle, tyre, bag)

At step 2 (the tanker in the strait), a large red "X" or disruption symbol shows where the chain breaks.

Below each step, show the price impact: "+74%", "+100%", "+74%", "+69%", "?", etc.

**Style:** Isometric or flat side-view silhouettes. Warm palette. Each step in a different viz color. Connecting lines flow left to right. The disruption point should be visually dominant — a crack, a barrier, a red zone.

**Size:** Full-bleed width (1200px). Aspect ratio ~6:1 landscape (long horizontal scroll on mobile).

## 4. Mine & Missile Threat Diagram

**Placement:** In the backstory section, near the discussion of Iran's asymmetric capabilities.

**What to show:**
Cross-section view of the strait (water profile from surface to seabed):
- **Surface:** VLCC tanker silhouette traversing
- **Above surface:**
  - Drone silhouette approaching from Iranian coast
  - Coastal missile trajectory arc from shore battery
- **At waterline:**
  - Fast attack boat (small, low profile) approaching
- **Below surface:**
  - Moored mine (floating just below surface on chain)
  - Bottom mine (sitting on seabed)
  - Depth markings: 25m near Iranian coast, 60m in channel
- **Shore (Iranian side):**
  - Missile battery on elevated terrain
  - Radar installation
  - IRGC fast-boat berth
- **Shore (Omani side):**
  - Relatively flat, minimal military infrastructure

**Style:** Clean cross-section diagram. Water in gradient from light (surface) to darker (depth). Threat vectors shown as subtle dotted lines in viz-red. Ship and weapons in clean silhouette. Educational, not sensationalist.

**Size:** Full breakout width (960px). Aspect ratio ~2:1 landscape.

## 5. Pipeline Bypass Map — The Inadequate Alternatives

**Placement:** In "The bypass gap" section, alongside the BypassGapChart.

**What to show:**
Simplified map of the Arabian Peninsula showing:
- **Strait of Hormuz** (marked as closed/disrupted with a red barrier)
- **Saudi East-West Pipeline:** From Abqaiq/Ras Tanura (Gulf coast) to Yanbu (Red Sea coast)
  - Label: "Spare capacity: 3-5M b/d"
  - Pipeline thickness proportional to capacity
- **UAE ADCOP Pipeline:** From Habshan to Fujairah (Gulf of Oman, bypassing the strait)
  - Label: "Spare capacity: 0.4M b/d"
  - Thinner line
- **Iran Goreh-Jask Pipeline:** From interior Iran to Jask (Gulf of Oman)
  - Label: "Capacity: 0.3M b/d"
  - Thin dashed line (new, small)
- **Total bypass capacity:** "~5M b/d" in large text
- **Total strait flow:** "20.9M b/d" in large text
- **The gap:** "~16M b/d — no backup" in accent color, very prominent
- Oil flow arrows showing crude destinations: China (~4.6M b/d), India (~2.1M b/d), Other Asia (~6.2M b/d), Europe (~0.6M b/d)

**Style:** Clean cartographic illustration. Land in warm sand/paper tones. Water in paper-alt. Pipelines as bold colored lines. The gap number should dominate the visual. Persian Gulf, Gulf of Oman, Red Sea, Arabian Sea all labeled.

**Size:** Full breakout width (960px). Aspect ratio ~1:1.

## 6. The Insurance Cost Stack — What It Costs to Cross

**Placement:** In the price cascade section, near the insurance discussion.

**What to show:**
A stacked bar / building-block diagram showing the cost layers of a single tanker voyage through Hormuz, before and after the crisis:

**Before (Feb 27):**
| Component | Cost |
|---|---|
| Charter/freight | $80,000/day |
| Fuel (bunker) | $437/tonne × 200 tonnes = ~$87,000 |
| War risk insurance | $250,000/voyage |
| Crew hazard pay | Minimal |
| **Total per voyage** | **~$1.2M** |

**After (March 7):**
| Component | Cost |
|---|---|
| Charter/freight | $486,000/day (4x) |
| Fuel (bunker) | $874/tonne × 200 tonnes = ~$175,000 (2x) |
| War risk insurance | $1,000,000+/voyage (4x) |
| Crew hazard pay | $500/day per crew |
| **Total per voyage** | **~$4.5M+** |

Show as two ships side by side — same vessel, wildly different cost stacks piled on top.

**Style:** Each cost layer as a colored block stacking on the ship. Before: calm blues. After: escalating warm colors (amber, red). Labels clear. The visual difference in height should be dramatic.

**Size:** Breakout width (960px). Aspect ratio ~3:2.

## 7. The Selective Closure — Who Gets Through

**Placement:** In the "How Iran closed the strait without closing it" callout area.

**What to show:**
A stylized top-down view of the strait entrance with ships queued:
- **Allowed through (green check):** Chinese tankers, select Indian vessels, Iranian crude carriers, Pakistan-flagged ships
- **Blocked (red X):** US-allied vessels, Western European tankers, Israeli-linked ships
- **Waiting (amber clock):** Japanese vessels (negotiating), Turkish ships, Saudi tankers
- **Anchored queue:** 200+ vessels shown as small dots outside the strait

Each ship category shows a flag icon and a brief status label. The visual makes it clear that Iran is *controlling* the strait, not physically blocking it.

**Style:** Clean bird's-eye diagram. Minimal detail. Ships as simple hull shapes. Color coding: green (allowed), red (blocked), amber (negotiating). The queue of waiting ships should feel crowded and tense.

**Size:** Breakout width (960px). Aspect ratio ~16:9.

## Production Notes

- All illustrations should be produced as SVG for crisp rendering at any size
- Provide 2x PNG fallbacks for older browsers
- Dark text labels use ink color; light text uses paper
- Every illustration needs a source line and a one-sentence editorial caption
- Mobile versions should stack or simplify where needed (especially #3 cascade chain)
- Consider subtle animation for the scrollytelling versions (ship moving through strait, cost blocks stacking up)
